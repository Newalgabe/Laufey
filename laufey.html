I grew up listening mostly to classical and jazz, and jazz music is where all my vocal inspiration has come from.

Raised between Reykjavík and Washington, D.C. by a musical family, Laufey Lín Jónsdóttir spent her childhood split between a cello case and a piano bench, absorbing Ella Fitzgerald and Chopin in equal measure.

She writes the way old jazz records used to feel - unhurried, a little bruised, dressed up in strings. Her arrangements borrow the swing and shimmer of the 1950s while her lyrics stay stubbornly, embarrassingly of-the-moment: crushes texted at 2am, love that doesn't call back.

What started as bedroom covers on the internet became sold-out arenas of people who never thought they'd love a torch song. Laufey didn't bring jazz back - she just proved it never really left.
